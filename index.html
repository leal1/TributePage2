Gordon Ramsay

sixteen Michelin star chef

Here is some information on Gordon

  • When Gordon was 19, he became serious about pursuing a culinary education and enrolled at North Oxfordshire Technical College to study Hotel Management.
  • In 1982, he worked at the Wroxton House Hotel and the Wickam Warms. He then moved to London, where he worked in numerous restaurants.
  • He worked for the Marco Pierre White at Harvey's for almost 3 years and he decided to study French Cuisine. White advised and referred Gordan to begin working for Albert Roux at Le Gavroche in Mayfair.
  • After working with Roux at Le Gavroche for a year, Gordon was invited to work with him at Hotel Diva, a ski resort in the French Alps, as his number two and later moved to Paris.
  • Gordon continued working in France for three years under the mentorship of Guy Savoy. Gordon then took the job as a personal chef on the private yacht, Idelwild, in the Bermudas.
  • In 1993, Gordon returned to London and was the head chef and 10% owner of Rossmore. The restaurant was renamed Aubergine and won a Michelon star.
  • Gordon Ramsey left Auberinge in 1997 to own and run his own restaurant in Chelsea. It was called Restaurant Gordon Ramsay, and it earned three Michelin stars in four years.
  • Gordon Ramsey then started a chain of successful restaurants in Glasgow, Ireland, Dubai, Tokyo, New York City, Florida, and Los Angeles, in the next 15 years.
  • He appeared on the television series, Faking It, in 2001, helping the prospective chef, a burger flipper named Ed Devlin, learn the trade. This episode won the BAFTA for Best Factual TV Moment.
  • Between 2004 and 2007, Ramsay appeared in the British television series, Ramsay's Kitchen Nightmares, aired on Channel 4, and saw the chef troubleshooting failing restaurants over a one week period.
  • In 2012, he opened The Fat Cow in Los Angeles, at The Grove, a shopping area popular with tourists that folks could go to all the time, relax and enjoy a terrific meal.
  • Hell's Kitchen, a reality show, on ITV1, saw Ramsay attempt to train ten British celebrities to be chefs in a restaurant on Brick Lane which opened to the public during the show.
  • He adapted Hell’s Kitchen to suit American audience in a series similar to the original British series and also went on to host a U.S version of Kitchen Nightmares between2007 and 2010.
  • In 2010, he was a producer and judge on the US version of MasterChef, starred in a travelogue about his visit to India, Gordon's Great Escape and hosted the series, Ramsay's Best Restaurant.
  • Till 2012, he penned 21 books and contributed articles to The Times Saturday magazine. Two of his books are his autobiographies, Roasting in Hell’s Kitchen and Humble Pie.
